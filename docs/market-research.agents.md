# Market research — "safety analysis as code" (STPA + SysML v2 + Lean 4)

Working notes, July 2026. Claims verified via web search where possible; unverified items flagged.

## 1. Buyer segments

**Automotive (ADAS/AV software safety teams).** Regulatory anchor: ISO 26262 (functional safety), ISO 21448/SOTIF (performance limitations), and UL 4600 for AV safety cases. STPA is not itself named by ISO 26262/21448, but is widely used as a complementary hazard-identification method feeding HARA, and is explicitly load-bearing inside UL 4600's safety-case methodology — combining STPA and HARA to extract loss scenarios and safety requirements from MBSE conceptual designs [Ansys UL4600 blog](https://www.ansys.com/blog/ul4600-making-the-case), [unified ISO26262/SOTIF/UL4600 framework](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S259019822500510X). Named targets: AV/robotaxi companies with in-house safety-case teams (Waymo, Cruise-successor orgs, Zoox), Tier-1s with dedicated functional-safety groups (Bosch, Continental, Aptiv), and eVTOL/UAM primes (Joby, Archer) that reuse automotive-style safety cases — Ansys explicitly markets ARP4754A/4761-style digital engineering to eVTOL OEMs [Ansys eVTOL blog](https://www.ansys.com/blog/design-safer-evtol-system-digital-engineering). Willingness-to-pay signal: these orgs already buy Ansys medini analyze and Vector/LDRA toolchains for ISO 26262 tool-qualification packages, so budget exists; the open question is whether they'd add a new CLI-based layer rather than extend medini. Adoption path: CI as a gate on pull requests that touch control-structure models, mirroring how DO-178C/ISO 26262 already require traceability evidence per change [traceability discussion](https://stell-engineering.com/blog/what-is-requirement-traceability).

**Aerospace/defense.** ARP4761A (Dec 2023 revision, harmonized with EUROCAE ED-135) is the certification-support safety-assessment standard; it does not mandate STPA, but MIT's Leveson group and industry papers position STPA as a complementary MBSA method that surfaces software/human-factors/operational hazards ARP4761 misses [MIT ARP4761-STPA comparison](http://sunnyday.mit.edu/papers/ARP4761-Comparison-Report-final-1.pdf), [STPA-as-complement summary](https://systemsafetyassessment.com/systems-theoretic-process-analysis-stpa/). Notably, SAE published **J3307-2025**, a cross-industry STPA execution standard (March 2025), which materially strengthens the "STPA is a citable, standardized method" pitch for RFPs and audits [SAE J3307](https://www.sae.org/standards/j3307_202503-system-theoretic-process-analysis-stpa-standard-industries), [ANSI listing](https://webstore.ansi.org/standards/sae/sae33072025). MIL-STD-882E governs DoD system safety broadly (software control categories, severity, level-of-rigor) but doesn't name STPA specifically [MIL-STD-882E overview](https://acqnotes.com/acqnote/tasks/mil-std-882e-system-safety). DO-178C/DO-330 tool qualification is directly relevant to the Lean-based type checker: NASA Langley ran an extensive study on qualifying open-source formal-methods tools (theorem provers, model checkers) under DO-330, finding qualification is feasible but historically rare because such tools rarely undergo it [NASA DO-330 case studies](https://shemesh.larc.nasa.gov/fm/FMinCert/DO-330-case-studies-RC.html), [NASA/CR-2017-219371](https://shemesh.larc.nasa.gov/fm/FMinCert/NASA-CR-2017-219371.pdf). Named buyers: primes with digital-engineering pushes (Lockheed, Northrop, Boeing), new-space companies, and DoD software-acquisition-pathway programs, which now target sub-annual delivery cycles — a cultural fit for CI-based tooling [DoD software acquisition pathway](https://www.airandspaceforces.com/software-acquisition-pathway-air-force-part-1/).

**Medical devices.** FDA's infusion-pump total-product-life-cycle guidance requires detailed hazard analysis in premarket submissions [FDA guidance](https://www.fda.gov/regulatory-information/search-fda-guidance-documents/infusion-pumps-total-product-life-cycle); FDA-funded research has extended STPA specifically for use-related hazards in infusion pumps (Generic Infusion Pump project) [RegDesk summary](https://www.regdesk.co/blog/fda-guidance-on-infusion-pumps-hazard-analysis/). IEC 62304 is mid-revision: Edition 2 (targeting publication mid/late 2026) collapses three safety classes into two process-rigor levels and adds explicit AI/SaMD scope [IEC 62304 Ed2 changes](https://intuitionlabs.ai/articles/iec-62304-edition-2-medical-software-changes) — a live regulatory-change moment that favors vendors who can demonstrate structured, versioned hazard traceability. Named targets: infusion-pump and SaMD startups, plus larger device makers (Medtronic, BD) with dedicated software-safety groups already using Jama Connect's ISO-14971-preconfigured hazard module [Jama risk management](https://www.jamasoftware.com/solutions/risk-management/).

**Rail.** EN 50126 (RAMS) and EN 50129 (signalling electronics safety case) require systematic hazard analysis and a structured Safety Case document [EN 50129 overview](https://safety-campus.de/en/standards-railway-safety-standards-en-50126-en-50129-en-50716/), but no search surfaced STPA being named in-standard — treat STPA-in-rail as (unverified) beyond scattered case studies. Rail is a plausible but lower-priority segment given long incumbent-tool lock-in (Siemens Rail Automation, Alstom in-house tooling).

**Nuclear.** Strongest unexpected finding: the NRC is actively evaluating STPA as substitute/complementary evidence for digital I&C diversity-and-defense-in-depth analysis, and NEI (2021) published STPA-based guidance for software common-cause-failure analysis; HAZCADS combines FTA+STPA and is gaining traction [NRC STPA investigation](https://www.nrc.gov/docs/ML2227/ML22272A315.pdf), [STPA-based NPP I&C analysis](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0951832021002349). This is a live regulatory tailwind — the NRC's own report title ("Investigation of the Use of System-Theoretic Process Analysis at the NRC") signals institutional buy-in forming right now.

**Space.** NASA has applied STPA extensively (space launch vehicles, MOXIE on Mars) via Leveson/Thomas's STPA Handbook, though no single "NASA STPA handbook" branded document was confirmed as an official NASA publication — the canonical handbook is MIT/Leveson-authored and NASA-funded case studies cite it [space launch STPA](http://sunnyday.mit.edu/Rising-JSSE.pdf), [MOXIE STPA](https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/8396586/). Treat "NASA handbook" framing as (unverified) — it's a Leveson-authored handbook NASA programs use, not a NASA-issued standard.

**Emerging AI-safety/autonomy assurance.** Active research area: multiple 2024-2026 papers propose STPA for frontier-AI hazard analysis and argue it should complement capability evals and safety cases [Systematic Hazard Analysis for Frontier AI using STPA](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2506.01782), [safety cases for frontier AI](https://arxiv.org/html/2410.21572v1). This is pre-commercial — funding/interest from AI-safety orgs (GovAI-adjacent, frontier labs' safety teams) but no procurement budget line yet; best treated as a thought-leadership/BD wedge, not near-term revenue.

**MBSE tool vendors as partners/acquirers/competitors.** SysML v2 finalized adoption by OMG (July 2025) [OMG SysML v2 adoption](https://www.omg.org/news/releases/pr2025/07-21-25.htm). Vendor landscape: Dassault/No Magic Cameo & CATIA Magic added SysML v2 support in 2026 (only specific bundle tiers) [GoEngineer](https://www.goengineer.com/blog/advantages-of-sysml-v2-now-available-in-no-magic-cameo-and-catia-magic-2026); Intercax Syndeia offers SysML v2 API-based digital threads [Intercax](https://www.omg.org/sysml/sysmlv2/sysml-tool/); Sensmetry (Syside/Sysand, targeting v1 status Q3 2026) is building out requirements-tool interop (Jama, Polarion, DOORS, Cameo) [Sensmetry roadmap](https://sensmetry.com/product-roadmap-2026-q2-update/); Obeo's SysON is open-source, web-native SysML v2 [SysON](https://mbse-syson.org/). These are natural integration partners (emit into their model stores) or, if any decides to build STPA/type-checking natively, competitors. None currently ship a Lean-grounded type checker — this is a differentiator, not yet a contested feature.

## 2. Competitive landscape

- **STAMP Workbench / XSTAMPP / A-STPA**: free, academic-origin STPA editors. XSTAMPP is open-source (Eclipse-based, plugin architecture: STPA, CAST) [XSTAMPP GitHub](https://github.com/asimabdulkhaleq/XSTAMPP); STAMP Workbench aimed at beginners with step-by-step guidance [tool comparison table](https://www.researchgate.net/figure/Comparison-of-WebSTAMP-STAMP-Workbench-SafetyHAT-and-XSTAMPP-taking-into-consideration_tbl1_331279389). No CI integration, no machine-checked traceability, no proof certificates — they're diagramming/documentation aids, not type systems. Pricing: free/academic (no vendor charges found).
- **STPAmaster**: newer commercial-leaning STAMP toolset positioning itself as "next generation" [STPAmaster](https://stpamaster.com/) — pricing not published (unverified).
- **Ansys medini analyze**: commercial, ISO-26262-oriented HAZOP/FTA/FMEA/FMEDA suite; custom/quote-based pricing, 30-day trial [Capterra](https://www.capterra.com/p/238340/ANSYS-Medini-Analyze/). No confirmed native STPA module in the sources found — an open gap it doesn't obviously fill; strong PLM/toolchain lock-in via Ansys/SCADE ecosystem is its moat, not STPA depth.
- **Jama Connect**: risk-management module preconfigured with ISO 14971 hazards, live traceability from hazard to mitigation to test, TÜV-SÜD validated for safety-related development [Jama risk mgmt](https://www.jamasoftware.com/solutions/risk-management/); pricing custom/quote-based, not published.
- **Polarion (Siemens)**: ALM/requirements competitor to Jama, comparable traceability story [Jama vs Polarion comparison](https://www.trustradius.com/compare-products/jama-connect-vs-polarion-alm); no STPA-specific module surfaced.
- **Common gap across all incumbents**: none machine-check totality/orphan-UCA-type conditions, none diff safety findings in CI/PR the way software-engineering CI gates work, and none ground findings in a proof-checked type system. This is the core white space this product targets.
- **Adjacent — assurance-case tooling**: Adelard ASCE is the dominant commercial GSN/CAE tool, GSN v3 support as of ASCE 5.1 [Adelard ASCE](https://www.adelard.com/asce/); AdvoCATE is an academic/NASA-Ames assurance-case automation toolset [AdvoCATE](https://link.springer.com/chapter/10.1007/978-3-642-33675-1_2). GSN export from this product would slot alongside, not replace, ASCE — potential integration point.

## 3. Reasons they might not want to work with us

- **Lean 4 unfamiliarity / hiring risk.** Safety engineering teams hire control-systems and reliability engineers, not theorem-prover experts; a small vendor asking a functional-safety team to trust (and eventually staff around) Lean 4 is a real hiring-pipeline risk. Overcome only if the CLI/report layer fully hides Lean from day-to-day users and the vendor commits to long-term support — a credibility problem for a small team with "no incumbent sales channel."
- **Tool qualification burden cuts both ways.** DO-330/ISO 26262 TCL qualification for a *new*, *open-source* prover is unproven territory: NASA's own study found formal-methods tools "have rarely undergone qualification" and documents the obstacles [NASA DO-330 study](https://shemesh.larc.nasa.gov/fm/FMinCert/NASA-CR-2017-219371.pdf). ISO 26262 Method 1a qualification-by-track-record requires "long-term, widespread use in comparable projects" [TCL methods](https://piembsystech.com/tool-qualification-tcl-iso-26262/) — a new toolchain has none. This is a first-mover cost the buyer, not the vendor, ultimately bears in an audit; DER/DAL-C-and-above programs and TCL2/3 automotive programs will balk until reference qualification packages exist.
- **STPA is recommended, not mandated, almost everywhere.** ARP4761A doesn't require STPA (complementary only); ISO 26262/SOTIF don't name it; MIL-STD-882E doesn't name it; EN 50126/50129 don't name it in the sources found. Only SAE J3307 (2025) is STPA-specific, and it's a "may be used" standard, not incorporated by reference into a certification basis anywhere yet (unverified whether any regulator cites J3307). Buyers who are audited against a checklist standard have limited incentive to adopt a method their auditor doesn't require — the sale has to be made on engineering-quality grounds, not compliance-forcing grounds, which is a slower, more skeptical buying motion.
- **SysML v2 tooling is early.** Industry commentary explicitly flags 2027–2028 as the realistic production-readiness window, and calls v1→v2 migration "high-risk, low-reward for mission-critical programs" today [SysML v2 critique](https://medium.com/@nfigay/sysml-v2-at-industrial-scale-why-success-depends-on-testbeds-not-technology-c77c3d1083fd). Betting the product's model layer on SysML v2 textual notation means riding an immature standard's adoption curve — buyers may defer even if they like the STPA/type-checking story, simply because they haven't moved off SysML v1/UML yet.
- **Incumbent bundling.** Orgs already paying for medini analyze, Jama, or Polarion have hazard-analysis "good enough" inside tools they already budgeted for and integrated with PLM/ALM. A point solution has to displace sunk procurement cost and integration effort, not just be better.
- **"Proofs" read as academic overclaim to auditors.** Certification auditors are trained to trust process evidence (review records, test logs, traceability matrices) over machine proofs they can't independently evaluate; a proof certificate from an unfamiliar prover may raise more questions ("who verifies the verifier?") than it answers, especially absent DO-330 qualification.
- **Small-vendor sustainability/liability risk.** A two-person-scale for-all.dev-type vendor asking a regulated safety org to depend on it for CI-gating safety findings is a real vendor-risk-assessment red flag — no enterprise support SLA, no proven multi-year continuity, and open-source-core means the buyer must ask "what if they disappear."
- **Conservative procurement cycles vs. startup runway.** A&D software-adjacent implementations run 12–24 months [ERP timeline](https://www.erpresearch.com/industries/manufacturing/aerospace-defense); DoD PALT reform is trying to shrink but hasn't yet [CSIS](https://www.csis.org/analysis/cycle-times-and-cycles-acquisition-reform). A small vendor's runway may not survive the sales cycle for its best-fit (most regulated, highest-willingness-to-pay) segments — a structural mismatch.
- **Git/CI workflow presumption.** "Analysis as code" presumes engineers work in git/PR/CI flows; many safety departments still work in siloed ALM tools (DOORS, Polarion) with change-control processes built around documents, not diffs. Convincing a safety department to adopt a git-centric workflow is itself a change-management sale, independent of the STPA/Lean pitch.
- **ITAR/export control.** GitHub.com is explicitly not designed to host ITAR-controlled data; compliant orgs need GitHub Enterprise Server self-hosted or GCC High-style isolated clouds [GitHub trade controls](https://docs.github.com/en/site-policy/other-site-policies/github-and-trade-controls), [ITAR cloud guidance](https://concentric.ai/itar-compliance-what-every-cio-and-cso-needs-to-know/). A CI-based SaaS offering is a non-starter for ITAR-covered defense programs unless it can run fully on-prem/air-gapped — this rules out cloud-hosted GitHub Actions as delivered "out of the box" for a meaningful slice of the aerospace/defense segment.
- **LLM-in-the-loop as liability, not feature.** In regulated contexts, an LLM suggesting analysis content — even "guarded by the type checker" — invites exactly the auditor skepticism above (opaque generation process feeding into safety artifacts). Framing it as engineer-productivity tooling with the type checker as the actual safety gate (LLM proposes, prover disposes) is necessary messaging discipline, not optional.

## 4. Wedges and counter-positioning

1. **New-space/eVTOL/AV startups already on GitHub.** These orgs (Joby/Archer-tier down to smaller UAM/robotics startups) already run software engineering in git/CI, are pre- or early-certification (so no legacy ALM lock-in to displace), and face UL 4600-style safety-case obligations where STPA is explicitly load-bearing. They're small enough that a founder-to-founder sales motion works, and technical enough to evaluate a Lean-based prover on its merits rather than reflexively distrusting it. Best first wedge.
2. **FDA pre-submission support for SaMD/infusion-pump startups riding IEC 62304 Edition 2.** The 2026 IEC 62304 revision is a live regulatory-change event; startups navigating it for the first time need help structuring hazard-to-mitigation traceability now, before old habits set in, and are small enough to adopt a CLI/CI-based workflow without an ALM-migration fight. Consulting-flavored engagement (help build the pre-sub package) is a lower-commitment entry point than "buy the toolchain."
3. **Research/consulting arm engagement with nuclear NRC's STPA push and AI-safety STPA research.** Both are pre-commercial but credibility-building: contributing tooling/analysis to the NRC's active STPA-for-digital-I&C evaluation, or to the frontier-AI-STPA research community, builds reference credibility and case studies usable to sell into automotive/aerospace later, without needing those buyers' 12–24 month procurement cycles yet. Treat as BD/thought-leadership, not near-term revenue — but valuable given "no incumbent sales channel."

Counter-positioning against objections: lead with "type checker as CI gate, LLM optional and always downstream of the checker" (defuses LLM-liability and auditor-overclaim concerns); ship an on-prem/self-hosted CI story from day one (defuses ITAR concern for wedge 1's eventual move upmarket); publish a DO-330/ISO-26262 qualification roadmap early even if unqualified today (shows awareness rather than ignorance of the tool-qualification gap); and target segments where STPA is standards-adjacent-but-not-mandatory as an engineering-quality sale, not a compliance-forcing sale, since compliance-forcing is not currently available as a lever (J3307 aside).
